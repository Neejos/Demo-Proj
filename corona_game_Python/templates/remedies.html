Foolproof Ayurvedic remedies to fight a cold or a flu


Simple home remedies to fight a cold or a flu

Is everybody around you catching a cold or a cough? Are you too on the verge of getting sick? Well, we don't blame you entirely! Just like the monsoon season, disease spreading germs and viruses are also refusing to leave! Once the nasty cold, cough or fever sets in, it takes its own time to go away and take a toll on your health. As important it is to limit your contact with the germs during this season, the #1 precautionary step you should be following is to boost your immunity naturally. If your body is healthy and protected, you are less prone to catching infections. There are also many Ayurvedic practices that can help you stay healthy this season. Plus, they are so easy to follow too!

Why Ayurveda can be the cure for your cold

As a branch of holistic healing, Ayurveda lays emphasis on synchronizing your body's needs with the changing seasons. Hence, a lot of Ayurvedic remedies have to do with the way you eat, cook, drink or use foods. Here are four such remedies which can come to your rescue:

Ayurvedic self-massage

The traditional healing system is built on the belief that our skin is host to a number of sensory neurons which hold the power to detox you naturally. Using certain oils to give yourself a massage can calm down the nervous system, strengthen immunity and also deal with other chronic infections

Oiling your ear

Ayurveda highly suggests you to make oiling your ear a part of your self-care routine. Putting some drops of traditional oils like mustard oil, sesame oil-the ones you can easily find in your home can work better your cold by lubricating your lymph nodes and even massage the immune system, making it function better. It also prevents cavities and passageways from drying, which can be an easy place for bacteria and viruses to hide. To start with, you can do this twice daily. Practicing oil pulling or swishing is another remedy to fight a bad viral infection. Ingesting herbal oils through the mouth can easily get rid of bad bacteria and boost the production of good bacteria in your body.

Turmeric paste to the rescue

Turmeric is a traditional spice which has been the go-to remedy to fight any infection! Our elders have been doing this for ages and recommending us to follow the same too. Consuming a simple turmeric paste made out of turmeric powder and raw organic honey can fill you up with antioxidants and vitamins, which fight bad viral strain. You can also try adding other wonderful kitchen ingredients like ghee, black pepper or mishri (rock sugar) to make the paste super potent and helpful.

Start taking ashwagandha

We don't need to tell you about the many benefits of ashwagandha. A true superfood, this Ayurvedic herb is now selling like hotcakes all around the west and contains magical powers of healing the body of infections and symptoms of cold, cough and viral bugs. While Ashwagandha should be consumed regularly to charge the body's immunity naturally, the onset of a sudden cold infection should make you increase the dosage of ashwagandha up to three times a day, either in powder or tablet form. The herb's natural immuno-boosting properties can also help you deal with chronic stress and fatigue which can come with viral infections.
